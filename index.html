Biography



Coco Gauff was born to Candy and Corey Guaff and has two younger brothers. Gauff started to begin to play tennis at the age of 6 turning eight she worked with Geral Logo at the New Generation Tennis Academy. Due to Gauff's heavy passion for tennis, her parents gave up their careers for her her father became her primary coach and her mother oversaw her homeschooling. At the age of 10, Gaurff would begin to train at Mouratoglou Academy in France. She would win the USTA Clay Court National 12 and under the title at the age of 10 and three months later would become the youngest champion in the tournament history. Throughout her junior career, she made it semifinals for the Les Petiis as a 14-and-under tournament in 2016, as well as becoming the youngest girl single finalist in the US Open history. She officially became a professional in 2018 at the age of 18 where she won her first professional match. Along with her becoming professional and beginning to play in the US Open, she began to raise the ranking becoming the top 100. Her accomplish continue but her biggest accomplish has been her recent win of winning her first Grand Slam at the US Open which made her rank number one but even bigger than at the age of 19
